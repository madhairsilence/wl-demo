Plain Language Summary Long COVID refers to symptoms that persist or emerge three months after a SARS-CoV-2 infection, lasting at least two months without another cause. It affects various body systems, causing fatigue, cognitive issues, breathing difficulties, and chest pain, with symptoms differing widely among people. The disease's complexity involves factors like viral persistence, immune dysfunction, and microbiome changes. Long COVID significantly impacts individuals' quality of life and increases healthcare costs and service demands. This review outlines the disease's definition, symptoms, mechanisms, and treatment progress, emphasizing the need for further research to better understand and manage long COVID.

Plain Language Summary This study explored how fatigue, depression, anxiety, and acute COVID-19 severity affect long-term health-related quality of life (HRQoL) in people with long COVID. Conducted in Portugal, it involved 208 adults who had COVID-19 at least 6 months prior. Results showed that fatigue, particularly physical fatigue, is strongly linked to depression and anxiety. Plain Language Summary This study highlights the limitations of comparing COVID-19 hospitalization outcomes based on vaccination status, as these groups may not be directly comparable for causal analysis. Using the principal stratification framework, the authors argue that such studies can only estimate the vaccine's causal effect for individuals who would be hospitalized regardless of vaccination. Plain Language Summary This study examined how COVID-19 vaccines affected patients with rheumatological diseases, focusing on their perception of protection and the incidence and severity of infections before and after vaccination. Researchers surveyed 407 patients, including those with immune-mediated inflammatory diseases (IMID) and musculoskeletal conditions, all of whom received at least two vaccine doses. The study highlights the importance of vaccination programs for infection prevention in these patients.
